Elkosh
Title sequence for a mock documentary centered on a fictional murder incident set in the 1970s in the moshav of Elkosh.

The sequence is constructed primarily through the editing and recontextualization of existing archival footage, combined with newly captured footage by me. This juxtaposition creates a visual dialogue between past and present, as well as between the contemporary landscape and the location where the imagined event is said to have taken place.

Through pacing, imagery, and atmosphere, the piece aims to evoke the tone and investigative language commonly associated with documentary cinema, while examining how editing and visual composition can shape narrative and perception. It is important to note that this project is a fictional exercise and does not depict or refer to a real murder case.
